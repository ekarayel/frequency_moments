\begin{isamarkuptext}%
Here we define upper (``above") and lower (``below") bounds operators. We
  think of \isa{r} as a \emph{non-strict} relation. The suffix \isa{S} at the names of
  some operators indicates that the bounds are strict -- e.g., \isa{underS\ a} is
  the set of all strict lower bounds of \isa{a} (w.r.t. \isa{r}). Capitalization of
  the first letter in the name reminds that the operator acts on sets, rather
  than on individual elements.%
\end{isamarkuptext}\isamarkuptrue%
